Project Overview

Objective:

Analyze customer data across multiple dimensions:

Demographic

Geographic

Payment & Account Info

Services

Study churner profiles and identify areas for targeted marketing campaigns.

Develop insights to predict future churners and improve customer loyalty.


 Tools Used:
 
Power BI – for data visualization and dashboard creation

SQL – for data extraction, transformation, and analysis

Power Query Editor – for data cleaning and ETL operations

DAX – for KPI and calculated measure creation


Key Metrics:

Total Customers: 6,418

New Joiners: 411

Total Churn: 1,732

Churn Rate: 27.0%


Additional Insights:

Highest Churn by State: Jammu (57.2%)

Highest Churn by Internet Type: Fiber Optic (41.1%)

Highest Churn by Contract Type: Month-to-Month (46.5%)

Churn by Gender: Female (64.15%) vs Male (35.85%)  



Analysis Summary:

Customers with Month-to-Month contracts show the highest churn (46.5%).

Fiber Optic users exhibit the greatest churn among internet types (41.1%).

Older customers (>50 years) have higher churn compared to younger age groups.

Mailed Check payment method users are more likely to churn (37.8%) than credit card users (14.8%).

Geographic analysis reveals Jammu and Assam have the highest churn rates.


Recommendations:

Encourage customers to move from month-to-month to annual or two-year contracts with discount offers.

Investigate Fiber Optic service quality issues to reduce dissatisfaction.

Implement loyalty programs targeting long-tenured and senior customers.

Promote digital payment methods through cashback or convenience benefits.

Address high-churn regions with localized marketing and retention campaigns.


Overall Conclusion:

The analysis demonstrates that customer churn is influenced by multiple factors such as contract type, payment mode, age group, and internet service type.
By acting on these insights — improving service quality, offering longer-term contracts, and enhancing customer experience — organizations can significantly reduce churn and boost retention.
